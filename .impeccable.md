## Design Context

### Users
Personal portfolio showcase — the primary viewer is someone evaluating technical and design skill. Visual craft matters above all else. Secondary audience is curious food/science people who might genuinely use it, but the aesthetic impression comes first.

### Brand Personality
Scientific, editorial, warm. FlavorNet sits at the intersection of rigorous food chemistry research and sensory pleasure — it should feel like a beautifully produced academic journal about gastronomy. Not a startup, not a dashboard, not a food blog. Something more considered.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light mode — warm parchment backgrounds (`#fdf6ec`), deep brown text (`#2d1b0e`), terracotta accent (`#c4622a`)
- **Typography**: Georgia serif for all display, headlines, and editorial text; system-ui sans for data labels, metadata, and UI controls
- **Visual character**: Editorial restraint with precise spacing. Thin borders as structural elements. No gradients, no shadows beyond subtle card lift.
- **Anti-references**: No dark mode dashboards, no neon accents, no glassmorphism, no gradient text, no rounded everything

### Design Principles
1. **Consistency is craft** — every pixel follows the system; deviations read as mistakes, not decisions
2. **Typography-first hierarchy** — size, weight, and spacing create structure; decoration doesn't
3. **Tokens everywhere** — never reach outside the design system for colors; warm palette holds all semantic meanings
4. **Left-aligned, editorial** — text anchors left; asymmetric breathing room feels intentional
5. **States are complete** — every interactive element has hover, focus, disabled, loading, and error states that feel resolved, not forgotten
